---

## What Is a Stand-up (Reminder)?

A **stand‑up** (or **daily Scrum**) is a **short, time‑boxed meeting** (5–15 minutes) where each person answers three questions:

1. **What did I accomplish since the last stand‑up?**  
2. **What am I planning to do next (today)?**  
3. **Is anything blocking my progress?**  

The goal is **alignment** and **early visibility of blockers**, not solving every problem in detail.

---

## Why Practice Stand-ups in Week 4?

In Week 4 we focus on **design deep dive and technical design**. In real projects:
- You connect **requirements → design → configuration → tests** in short cycles.
- Daily stand‑ups help track progress on design documents, decisions, and open questions.
- Many SAP projects use **SAP Activate + agile delivery**, which relies on regular stand‑ups.

Practicing this now makes it easier to fit into real project teams later.
