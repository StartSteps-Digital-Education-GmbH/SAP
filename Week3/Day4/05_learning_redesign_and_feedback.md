# Day 4: Re‑Designing Based on Customer Feedback
## Using Feedback to Improve Your SAP Design

In this learning file you will explore how to handle **feedback** on your SAP solution design and when to adjust or re‑design parts of the solution.

---

## 🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will:
- Explain why **feedback loops** are critical in SAP design
- Describe key components of **re‑designing** based on feedback
- Recognize typical **elements** in feedback handling (feedback collection, analysis, change decisions)
- Understand how good feedback practices support **trust** and **project success**

---

## Part 1: Why Feedback Matters

Even a well‑prepared design pitch will:
- Not answer **every question**
- Reveal **new information** or constraints
- Trigger **concerns** about cost, timeline, or risk

Feedback helps you:
- Improve the design before it is too late
- Align the solution with **real priorities**
- Build **credibility** as a consultant who listens

Ignoring or minimizing feedback can lead to:
- Resistance during implementation
- Scope conflicts
- Lower adoption after go‑live

---

## Part 2: Components of Re‑Design Based on Feedback

You can think of three main **components**:

### 1. Feedback Collection

- Capture feedback from:
  - Design review meetings
  - Workshops with users
  - Follow‑up emails and comments
- Use simple structures:
  - What is the **concern**?
  - Which **area** of the design does it affect?
  - How **urgent** or important is it?

### 2. Analysis and Prioritization

- Group feedback into categories, for example:
  - **Must change** (critical issues or risks)
  - **Should change** (important improvements)
  - **Could change** (nice‑to‑have ideas)
- Consider:
  - Impact on **scope, cost, and timeline**
  - Impact on **business value** and **risk**

### 3. Re‑Design and Decision

- Decide what to:
  - **Change** in the design
  - **Clarify** (communication issue, not a design issue)
  - **Park** for later phases or future releases
- Update:
  - Design documentation and diagrams
  - Action items and backlogs
  - Stakeholders (what changed and why)

---

## Part 3: Elements – Feedback Loops, Change Management, Validation

Slides emphasize **feedback loops** and **change management**. In practice this includes:

| Element | What it includes | Why it matters |
|--------|------------------|----------------|
| **Feedback loops** | Regular moments where stakeholders can react to designs and demos | Prevents late surprises and improves solution fit |
| **Change management** | Communicating and managing design changes, including impacts | Keeps everyone aligned and reduces confusion |
| **Testing and validation** | Checking that re‑designed parts actually solve the problem | Ensures changes are effective and do not create new issues |

Examples:
- Updating a process design after pilot users report that a step is confusing
- Adjusting security roles after IT raises concerns
- Simplifying scope for phase 1 after executives raise timeline concerns
