# Day 1: Daily Stand-up & Scrum Practice
## What Is Scrum? What Is a Stand-up? Why Practice It?

Start each day with this short **stand-up** practice. On Day 1 we also introduce **Scrum** and the **stand-up** so you know why we do this every day.

---

## What Is Scrum?

**Scrum** is an **agile framework** for getting work done in small, time-boxed iterations called **sprints**. Teams plan a short period of work (e.g. 1–2 weeks), do the work, then review and adjust. Key ideas:

- **Transparency:** Everyone can see what is done, in progress, or blocked.
- **Inspection:** The team regularly checks progress (e.g. daily stand-up, sprint review).
- **Adaptation:** The team adjusts plans based on what they learn.

Scrum is widely used in software and SAP projects (e.g. with SAP Activate, agile delivery). Practicing it during training helps you work in real project teams later.

---

## What Is a Stand-up?

A **stand-up** (or **daily Scrum**) is a **short, time-boxed meeting** (usually 5–15 minutes) where each person answers three questions:

1. **What did I accomplish since the last stand-up?**
2. **What am I planning to do next (today)?**
3. **Is anything blocking my progress?**

The meeting is often done **standing** to keep it short. It is not for solving problems in detail – those are taken offline. The goal is **alignment** and **quick identification of blockers**.

---

## Why Is It Necessary to Practice It?

- **Habit:** In real projects, stand-ups happen every day. Practicing now builds the habit.
- **Clarity:** Writing your answers helps you plan the day and spot blockers early.
- **Communication:** Sharing with peers (or your future team) improves transparency and collaboration.
- **SAP relevance:** Many SAP implementations use agile and Scrum; consultants often join daily stand-ups.
