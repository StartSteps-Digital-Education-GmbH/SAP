## Launchpad, App Library, and Typical Use Cases

In this learning file you will look at how SAP Fiori is used **in practice** to support real business processes.

## Part 1: Fiori Launchpad in Daily Work

The **Fiori Launchpad** is the main entry point into Fiori apps. In daily work, users:
- Log in and see tiles or cards relevant to their **role**
- Click tiles to open apps that support their tasks
- May personalize which tiles they see

Examples:
- A **buyer** sees:
  - “Manage Purchase Orders”
  - “Approve Purchase Requisitions”
  - “Supplier Evaluation” apps
- A **sales manager** sees:
  - “Sales Overview” dashboard
  - “Manage Sales Orders”
  - “Customer Fact Sheet” apps

The goal is to:
- Reduce **navigation time**
- Keep users focused on **key tasks**

---

## Part 2: Fiori App Library – Conceptual Use

The **Fiori App Library** (online catalog) helps you:
- Find standard Fiori apps available for S/4HANA
- See **what each app does**, which roles it supports, and technical info
- Check prerequisites like scope items or back‑end components

In design discussions, you can:
- Search for apps that match a **business requirement**
- Decide whether a **standard app** is enough or if you need:
  - Customizing/configuration only
  - Extensions or custom apps using Fiori elements and BTP

---

## Part 3: Typical Fiori Use Cases

Some common ways Fiori is used in S/4HANA projects:

- **Manager approvals**
  - Approve purchase orders, sales orders, time sheets from mobile
- **Operational dashboards**
  - Monitor sales, procurement, or production KPIs
- **Self‑service**
  - Employees check pay slips, time entries, or request approvals
- **Fact sheets**
  - Quick overviews of customers, materials, or vendors with links to related apps

These use cases:
- Improve **user experience**
- Can often be delivered using **standard Fiori apps**
- Are aligned with Coursera topics on **customer and user experience**

---

## Part 4: Fit‑to‑Standard and Fiori

When designing with Fiori, you should ask:
- Is there a **standard Fiori app** that meets most of the need?
- Can we adapt standard apps with:
  - Configuration
  - Personalization
  - Minor extensions
- Only then consider **fully custom** apps

This supports:
- Faster implementations
- Easier maintenance and upgrades
- Better alignment with SAP **best practices**
