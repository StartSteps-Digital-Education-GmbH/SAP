## Part 1: What Is SAP Fiori?

**SAP Fiori** is SAP’s design system and UX approach for building modern, role‑based applications.

Key ideas:
- **Role-based** – users see what they need for their role (e.g. approver, accountant, buyer)
- **Responsive** – works on desktop, tablet, and mobile
- **Simple** – focus on key tasks and information, not complex screens

Benefits:
- Better **user adoption** and satisfaction
- Faster task completion and fewer errors
- Easier onboarding for new users

---

## Part 2: Fiori Components – Fiori Elements and Launchpad

### Fiori Elements

- Provide **predefined templates** for common app types (list reports, object pages, overview pages, etc.)
- Ensure:
  - Consistent look and feel
  - Reuse of UI patterns and components
  - Faster development of new apps

### Fiori Launchpad

- The **entry point** for Fiori apps
- Users see:
  - Tiles and cards representing apps and key information
  - A layout tailored to their **role**
- Supports:
  - Personalization (which tiles to show)
  - Navigation to apps and links

---

## Part 3: Fiori App Types and Examples

Three common **Fiori app types**:

| App type | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Transactional app** | Supports day‑to‑day tasks and updates | Approve purchase orders, create sales orders |
| **Analytical app** | Provides dashboards and insights on KPIs | Sales performance dashboard, overdue items |
| **Fact sheet** | Shows detailed information about a specific business object | Customer or material fact sheet |

When designing a solution, you should think:
- Which **roles** need which **apps**?
- Do they need **transactional**, **analytical**, or **fact sheet** views (or a mix)?

---

## Part 4: What Is the Simplification List?

The **Simplification List** is SAP’s catalog of changes, improvements, and deprecations when moving to S/4HANA.

It helps you see:
- Which **transactions and functions** have changed or been removed
- Which **new capabilities** replace older ones
- Impacts on **custom code** and existing processes

Why it matters:
- Guides **fit‑to‑standard** decisions
- Helps identify required **process changes**
- Highlights potential **risks** and testing needs during migration

As a Technology Consultant, you will not memorize the entire list but you must:
- Know that it exists and where to find it
- Use it to **check impacts** on your design
