## Part 1: What Is RISE with SAP?

**RISE with SAP** is SAP’s offering that helps customers **move to the cloud and transform their business** with a guided approach.

In practice, RISE typically includes a combination of:
- **Cloud ERP** (often SAP S/4HANA Cloud as the core)
- **Business process transformation** support (how processes should change, not just the system)
- **Technology platform and innovation** capabilities (commonly via SAP **BTP**)
- Support for running and operating the solution in a cloud context

Think of it as:
> “A packaged way to modernize the customer’s SAP landscape, moving to cloud and improving processes, with SAP providing a structured path and services.”

---

## Part 2: Where RISE Fits in the Implementation Lifecycle

You can place RISE across multiple lifecycle phases:

- **Project preparation**
  - Scope the transformation, define goals, align stakeholders
  - Decide what “success” means (cost, speed, standardization, agility)

- **Blueprinting / design**
  - Fit‑to‑standard thinking is critical here
  - Clarify what is standard, what needs extension, and what is truly custom

- **Realization**
  - Configure and build the solution
  - Implement required integrations and extensions (often through BTP)

- **Final preparation + Go‑live**
  - Execute cutover, ensure readiness, stabilize operations
  - Establish monitoring and support processes

---

## Part 3: RISE and Your Earlier Topics (Week 3–4)

RISE connects strongly with:
- **Cloud strategy** (why cloud, what model, governance)
- **Fit‑to‑standard vs custom** (avoid unnecessary customization)
- **Technical design and integration** (what must connect, how, and with what tools)
- **ALM and operational readiness** (how the solution will be monitored and supported)

As a consultant, your job is to translate:
- Business goals → solution approach  
- Transformation goals → design decisions and implementation plan

---

## Part 4: Quick Example (Consultant View)

A customer says:
> “We want to move to cloud quickly and reduce IT operations overhead.”

In a RISE-based approach, you would likely:
- Push for **fit-to-standard** processes where possible
- Plan integrations and extensions carefully (avoid “custom by default”)
- Define a realistic **testing and cutover** approach early
- Ensure **monitoring, support, and roles** are clear before go‑live
