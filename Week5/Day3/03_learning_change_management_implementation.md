# Day 3: Change Management During Implementation
## Adoption Is a Deliverable, Not a Side Task

Implementation success is not only technical correctness—it also depends on whether users adopt the new way of working.

This short lesson ties change management into the implementation lifecycle, testing preparation, and go‑live readiness.

---

## 🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end, you will be able to:
- Explain why change management must start early (not at the end)
- Identify key change management activities that support testing and go‑live
- Describe what “good readiness” looks like for users and support teams

---

## Part 1: Where Change Management Fits in the Lifecycle

Change management spans the full lifecycle:
- **Project preparation**: stakeholder alignment and communications plan
- **Blueprinting/design**: involve key users, validate processes, manage expectations
- **Realization**: prepare training materials and support model, gather feedback
- **Final preparation**: training delivery, UAT participation, cutover communications
- **Go‑live**: hypercare support and quick issue handling
- **Continuous improvement**: ongoing learning and incremental adoption

---

## Part 2: Change Management Helps Testing (Not Only Go-Live)

Testing is stronger when:
- Key users understand the new processes
- Test scenarios reflect real work
- Defects are described clearly (business impact, not only “it doesn’t work”)

UAT fails when:
- Users are unprepared
- Roles/access are not ready
- Training is delayed

---

## Part 3: What a Simple Change Plan Includes

At minimum:
- **Communication plan** (who needs to know what, when)
- **Training plan** (who is trained, on what, with what materials)
- **Support model** (who supports users after go‑live, how to report issues)
- **Readiness checkpoints** (how you know people are ready)
