## Part 1: Two Types of SAP Modules

SAP modules are often grouped into two categories:

| | **Functional modules** | **Technical modules** |
|---|------------------------|------------------------|
| **Focus** | Specific business processes and operations | Development, integration, system administration |
| **Examples** | Finance, HR, Sales, CRM, Procurement | ABAP development, integration, system monitoring |
| **Who uses them** | Business users, process experts | Developers, IT, system admins |
| **Question they answer** | ‚ÄúHow do we run our business processes?‚Äù | ‚ÄúHow do we build, connect, and run the system?‚Äù |

## Part 2: Example ‚Äì Customer Relationship Management (CRM)

**CRM** = Managing relationships with customers (sales, marketing, service).

### Components (building blocks)

| Component | What it does |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Sales Force Automation** | Manages sales processes, opportunities, and pipelines |
| **Marketing** | Plans and runs marketing campaigns |
| **Service** | Manages customer service and support |
| **Interaction Center** | Handles customer interactions across channels (phone, email, chat) |
| **Field Service Management** | Manages service delivery in the field (e.g. technicians) |

### Functionalities (what the system can do)

- Customer data management  
- Sales and service automation  
- Marketing campaign management  

### Elements (data and objects)

- **Customer accounts** ‚Äì information on customers and their interactions  
- **Service tickets** ‚Äì records of customer service requests  
- **Campaigns** ‚Äì marketing initiatives and promotions  

### Practical application

- Better customer relationships and satisfaction  
- More efficient sales and service processes  
- Better tracking and effectiveness of marketing

## Part 3: Technical Modules ‚Äì Short Overview

**Technical modules** focus on:

- **Development** ‚Äì e.g. programming (ABAP), building apps  
- **Integration** ‚Äì connecting SAP to other systems  
- **System administration** ‚Äì keeping the system running, secure, and performant  

We will go deeper into technical topics later in the week; for now it is enough to know they are the ‚Äúhow we build and run the system‚Äù side.

## üéâ Key Takeaways

- **Functional modules** = business processes (Finance, HR, Sales, CRM, etc.).  
- **Technical modules** = development, integration, administration.  
- **CRM** is a functional module: components (e.g. Sales, Marketing, Service), functionalities (customer data, campaigns), elements (accounts, tickets, campaigns).  
- **Industry solutions** (e.g. Retail, Healthcare, Automotive) adapt SAP to sector-specific needs.
